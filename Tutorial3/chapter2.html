Chapter Two

The Rolling Cart

Again, I saw the king’s men, elegantly dressed, mounting their well decorated horses after a drink from the lake, I normally wait to see them pass through this path as they make their rounds watching the city. After every three sun sets, a new team will pass. They are bold and strong and make us feel safe. I particularly like the leader of the Pack. He rides in front and leads the way. He tells them when to stop and what to do. “One day I will lead that pack Harry said to himself, “and protect the city too, I will lead an army of men”. Moments later, as I walked down on the foot path near the sloppy plains on my way home, I heard loud screaming. I saw one horse running away in a distance without a rider and the other horses making snorting noises. I heard loud voices and what sounded like men fighting and so I quickly hid behind a small rock and watched unnoticed. The king’s men had been ambushed by hoodlums. The fight was fierce but the hoodlums outnumbered the king’s men. Only the leader of the pack was still standing and it looked like he will give in soon as he had three men fighting him alone. Wandering what to do to help, I looked around and the only thing in sight was a wooden cart supported by two big stones, so it won’t roll down the slope. I removed the two stone and let the cart roll from the top of the slope towards the fighting men and in a sudden panic from the noise of the uncontrollable cart rolling fast towards them and not sure what to expect, the hoodlums abandoned the fight and fled leaving the kings men by themselves. The kings men looked up in the direction of the rolling cart saw me standing and realized what happened, they called out to me to join them.
They thanked me for saving their lives as some of the soldiers were seriously wounded. While they nursed their wounds, the leader of the pack engaged me in a conversation and commended me for being such a brave lad. I went on to tell him stories of how I killed a bear and a Lion when they tried to attack my sheep and he was fascinated and called the others to come and hear my stories.  I also told them of my dream to join the king’s army in the future. The soldiers looked at him in shock and the head of the pack turned and addressed him as he would a superior officer and said, “One day, you will lead the army to victory and protect the people you love so much”. I kept his words safely fastened in my heart as I added today’s episode in my chronicles of fights and waiting for the next.
David knew he was a warrior and a leader of his people and he had an eye for the rulership. Even though he was just a shepherd boy. He was later anointed by Samuel but he knew it was a long journey. He always looked for opportunities that added value to his campaign, while waiting for the appointed time to lead his people. That was the same reason he fought goliath, it took him closer to his dream.
So that is the attitude you should have when you are waiting for your dreams to come true.

The Adventure

When you discover or begin to understand what problem you are wired to solve and you begin to take steps in that direction, a fresh consciousness begins to grow in you that you are now living on purpose and this brings a sense of fulfillment to you. The fact that every day is part of Gods big picture makes the day promising and exciting and that’s why it’s an adventure. The most exciting thing about an adventure is that you have an aim, something you want to achieve at the end of your expedition. Regardless of the challenges you come across, you are still focused. That is the whole idea, even when you are faced with circumstances that are not so pleasant; it becomes easy to see why you have to be strong and resolute.  God designed your adventure as part of His plan for you to be full of support for your growth and development.  The need to then remain close to God to keep you on track in your adventure becomes very important. It affects the things you say and the things you do because you don’t want anything that will derail you from the plan that God has for you. In the same vein, you live your life wisely acknowledging that you are on a journey and the success of that journey depends on you.  Your adventure is not a timeless period of uncertainty and confusion. It is phased into times and seasons, tailor made for you to fulfill purpose as will be discussed in the next topic.

Time and Phases

The journey of purpose takes a life time. God only reveals the big picture or the blueprint to you so you can have an idea because if He told you everything, you will not be able to handle it. Hence, He wants you to depend on Him for directions on what to do per time. This is because:

  • God is all knowing; he knows the details of your future and that of everyone else in the world.
  • God wants you to develop complete trust in Him with your life because He knows what is best for you.

That is why the adventure in fulfilling your assignment is phased over time; God’s plan for you is divided into basically two seasons
1. Preparation
2. Manifestation
God will direct and guide you in carrying out his plan which is your assignment. He will lead you to start the preparation and when you are ready, He will lead you to the place of assignment to kick off. God has everything planned in phases and he will communicate it to you just in time. All God demands from you is your attention and obedience. Once you have discovered the problem you are designed to solve, the next thing is to prayerfully seek development in that direction. Read books, attend trainings and seminars and associate with people in that circle. God will definitely order your steps to every place of preparation that you will need in carrying out your assignment. A wise man once said that when preparation meets with opportunity, you have a manifestation, what I like about this statement is the fact that God creates opportunities any time He likes and that makes me feel safe and encouraged to always be prepared.

Stay at your Post

It is important to note that as you step out preparing to fulfill your assignment,   there will be challenges, distractions and even discouragements. These are normal and should only make you stronger. If you turn away, you will live to face them another day. Your assignment will not change even if you runaway. So, be determined to face it and succeed. Stay committed to your call even when you are not sure of what next to do; just keep your hands busy and your heart connected to God. Consistency is of absolute necessity when it comes to fulfilling your assignment. Let God always find you at your post and let there be traces of you and your activities around your assignment.  As much as possible let the things you commit to connect to your assignment in one way or another.  There is always a cost that comes with fulfilling your God given dream; it may be your time, energy, sleepless nights, study research and even disappointments. But when you realize that it’s all part of the package, you are encouraged to continue. So, when you get to one of such days remember I told you, days like that will come. All you need to do is just face them and God will help you overcome them and you will be fine. Remain at your post and don’t wander away. It’s part of the adventure and it prepares you to stand firm in tough times that lie ahead.
