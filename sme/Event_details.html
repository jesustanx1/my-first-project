Rules, Regulation and Information for Participation in the SME Equipment Exhibition 2014

  1. ORGANISERS OF THE EXHIBITION

    The SME Equipment Exhibition 2014 event in Nigeria is organized and staged by FARMHAVILAH in collaboration with the Kaduna State chapter of Nigeria Association of Small and Medium Enterprises (NASME).
    The exhibition offers unique meeting points for Nigerian and Foreign Small and Medium equipment, Manufacturers, Suppliers, Buyers and Users, as well as opportunities for investment and promotion

    .

  2. DATE AND VENUE

    The Fair will be staged on Wednesday 26th through Friday 28th November 2014 at the Kaduna International Trade and Investment Centre, Kaduna - Zaria Road, Kaduna, the Capital City of Kaduna State, Nigeria.


  3. BUSINESS HOURS

    • The opening day ceremony of the fair will be performed on Wednesday 26th November, 2014 by His Excellency the Executive Governor of Kaduna State Alhaji Ramallan Yero. The time will be 10.00am and is strictly by invitation. Participants will be provided with invitation cards.
    • The Fair will be opened daily for business from the hours of 9.00am to 6.00p.m.
    • Delivery or removal of goods from the Exhibition Centre through the means of vehicles MUST be made between the hours of 6.00am to 8.00am daily in the morning and between 6.00pm and 7.00pm daily in the evening
    • No vehicle is allowed to park at the Exhibition Ground or walkways and access roads after construction period.
    • No delivery vehicles will be allowed into the Trade Fair Complex after hours reserved for delivery goods.

  4. OBJECTIVES OF THE EXHIBITION
    • To promote and encourage the development of small and medium industrial concerns.
    • To grow the National economy through SMEs and create job opportunities
    • To promote, encourage and expose Nigerians to small renewable energy projects.
    • To attract International Investment in production in line with the Federal Government policies.
    • To create conducive atmosphere between Nigeria and other Nations for joint venture projects in industrial sector.
    • To provide access to research findings, new technologies and ideas for actualization by industrialists and policy makers through face-to-face interaction.

  5. REQUEST FOR PARTICIPATION

    Applications for participation in the SME equipment exhibition 2014 are obtainable online or at the exhibition secretariat:

    Nigeria Association of Small Medium Enterprises. Kaduna State Chapter 4th Floor, Nagwamatse House 4/5 Ahmadu Bello Way Kaduna-Nigeria. Tel.: +234 (0)8069224080, +234 (0)8099631654, +234 (0)8087263911, +234 (0)8162006991. Website: http://www.smeequipex.com


  6. TERMS OF REFERENCE DEFINITION

    • The term 'Exhibition shall include all employees', servants and agents of any company, partnership, firm, government or individual to whom space is allocated for the purpose of show.
    • The term Organizers shall mean: - Nigerian Association of Small Medium Enterprises and Farmhavilah.
    • The term 'Contract' shall mean the condition for allocation of space at the Exhibition entered into between the Organizers and Exhibitors, which incorporates terms of contract and any rules, and regulations that may be subsequently issued.

  7. APPLICATION FOR PARTICIPATION

    • Completed forms should be returned to the office of the head, SME Equipment Exhibition 2014 with full payment for the services required. All payments are to be made online.
    • Any entry after 7th November, 2014 will not appear in the participation list of the Exhibition catalogue. Space is allocated on first come first serve basis & in accordance with the category of exhibition goods.

  10. PROHIBITED AND UNDESIRABLE EXHIBITS

    • No radio or sound equipment will be allowed to be used in all the exhibition halls/stands, except for demonstration purpose only. The sound MUST be minimal and limited within the four-walls of the Exhibitors pavilion. Any Exhibitor who violates this will have their gadgets/equipment confisticated.
    • Display or sales of immoral videocassette by exhibitors is not allowed.
    • Hawking of goods of any kind is prohibited in Exhibition ground.
    • The Trade Fair Committee prohibits the display or sales of the following articles during the Fair:
      • . Fire Arms and Ammunition
      • Radioactive products
      • All items banned by the Federal and State Government
      • Sales of alcoholic drinks by hoteliers or cafeterias are not allowed. However, Breweries are free to exhibit and promote their products, but not on retails or on the spot consumption.

  11. ALLOCATION AND CONSTRUCTION OF STANDS AND PAVILIONS

    • The Exhibition is partly OPENED and partly COVERED. Exhibitors are to erect and decorate their stands and pavilions (in the case of open space) by themselves. In the alternative, they could request the Committee to organize the erection of stands for them depending on the application requirements and taste.
    • The Exhibition Committee shall allocate space in accordance with the nature of the exhibits, goods or in manner it deem fit. The Exhibition Committee shall reserve the right to change the space allocated to an Exhibitor at any time prior to the commencement of erection of stand. Should exceptional circumstances so demand, such changes should be at the discretion of the Exhibition Manager and the Exhibitors shall have no claims for damages whatsoever.
    • . Exhibitors shall not store or dispose packaging materials in such a way as will constitute a nuisance. Obstruct free movement of people and traffic or disfigurement of the environment around their stand.
    • Exhibitors are not allowed to sublet the stand allocated to them to other parties either wholly or in part without the written consent of the fair Committee.
    • Construction of stands should be completed 7 days to the opening of the Exhibition.
    • The organizers reserve the right to determine the locations and grouping of Exhibitors on the basis of exhibits groups.
    • Participants are responsible for any probable damages or changes made on the pavilion. Otherwise the organizers are authorized to confiscate the exhibitor's deposits or wares of equivalence.

    • The Police Post in the Exhibition Arena shall remain opened 24 hours a day during the period of the exhibition and three days after the closure of the Fair.
    • However, individual Exhibitors may wish to engage the services of private security agencies for their private requirements and inform the organizers accordingly
    • However, the organizers shall not be responsible for any loss or damage to exhibits in the Exhibition Stands.

    • Full list of goods, showing identification mark Number description, quality, weight and CIF Value shall be furnished to the Customs at the port of entry.
    • . Further information relating to Customs formalities may be obtained from the Kaduna Chamber of Commerce Secretariat at Kaduna. Zaria Road, Kaduna, Embassies, High Commission abroad and Embassies of the participating countries in Nigeria.

  16. . SALES OF EXHIBITS

    • . Imported Exhibits/goods should not be sold until the close of Fair and subject to the compliance with the Nigeria Customs and Excise regulation.
    • Full details of business proposals, joint-ventures and sales exhibit goods should be made to the Exhibition Secretary for further assistance that may be required from the Government Agencies.

  17. . IMMIGRATION AND AIRPORT FORMALITIES

    • All persons wishing to enter Nigeria for the purpose of participating in the Exhibition either as an Exhibitor or visitor shall be required to obtain Entry Visa from Nigeria organizers as request.
    • Other immigration and airport formalities to be complied with include the possession of valid health certificate in respect of yellow Fever and valid return ticket.

  18. . ADVERTISING

    • . On site, the Exhibitor's name, sign and banner shall be displayed within the confines of their stands and should not protrude into corridors. Person caught in such act will have their goods confiscated.
    • An Exhibitor may install and use advertising equipment in their stand or display unites provided no disturbances are caused to other Exhibitors

  19. FAIR CATALOGUE

    • Official Exhibition Catalogue will be published by the Exhibition Committee.

  20. CAR PARK

    Suitable parking lots with necessary security have been provided around the Trade Fair Arena. Parking of vehicles shall therefore not be allowed in the Exhibition Arena. All vehicles are parked at owner's risk at the car park provided. No vehicle will be allowed to remain at the Exhibition Arena and any Car found parked or left on the arena for whatever reason outside the hour specified for delivery will be penalized N10, 000.00 per day otherwise Police may be required to tow away such vehicle.


  21. CARTERING FACILITIES

    Catering facilities will be provided within the Exhibition Arena and at the International standard hotels within easy reach or at other hotels of equal standard scattered within Kaduna Township.

  22. USE OF FAIR SPACE

    Exhibitors are bound to exhibit the announced products and services and should man the stand with capable personnel during the business hour of the Trade Fair.


  23. UTILITIES HANDLING

    • . Participants should retain their electricity take-off point to those authorized. Unauthorized addition of new points is dangerous and will attract immediate penalty of N10, 000.00 daily.
    • Under no circumstance should Exhibitors or their agents cut and tap underground cables. This could be highly dangerous.
    • . Exhibitors should be required to settle their space rates in full before taking possession of their stand, failure to comply with any of the aforementioned payment will result in delay or cancellation of the proposed space allocation.
    • . Free access road must be allowed for fire control equipment and water dowers.
    • There are regular refuse disposal facilities around the exhibition ground. Use them and no Exhibitor is allowed to burn refuse or dumps them outside their stand.

  24. . SUPPLEMENT CLAUSES

    Whenever necessary the organizers shall have the right to issue supplement regulations in the interest of Exhibitors and the additional written regulation instruction shall form part of the condition for participation and they shall be binding on the Exhibitors.
