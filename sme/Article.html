'Poor Storage Facilities for Tomatoes is Our Biggest Problem' - Mu'azu Datti Umar

Funding is not the priority of our association but availability of a tomato processing industry that can buy our produce in bulk to avoid losses encountered during harvest and storage. This statement is credited to Mu'azu Datti Umar, Chairman Kano State tomato farmers association, in a recent newspaper interview with 'Daily Trust'. This is generally the present situation between agro commodity output and low processing capacity. Farmers cannot be encouraged to increase output if they cannot guarantee sales outlets for their produce to improve their family economy and by extension the nation. One major step towards reversing this trend is provide options to add value to their commodities by processing into semi-finished and finished products. This will encourage them to increase yield as they are sure of a market and better premium thereby improving the economy, create employment and reduce poverty. It is our strong belief that our agricultural economy will not improve by the establishment of sporadic large scale farms but in multiples of small scale holders that could be made integral parts of contract farming for processors and industries. Overcoming post harvest losses, enhancing nutrition and adding value to agricultural produce through processing are the main ways of ensuring national food security. Available technology for processing limit these crops from reaching their full potential as sources of food and income. The development and introduction of new processing and post harvest storage technologies offer potential to improve food security and industrialization for economic growth. Herein lies the essence of this exhibition
